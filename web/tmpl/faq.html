Frequently Asked Questions

How Do I Sign Up For Scouts?

Registering for Scouts is easy! Visit scouts.org.nz to register online or call toll-free 0800-SCOUTS to be directed to the office closest to you.

How Do I Purchase a Uniform, and What Will My Child Need?

You can view and purchase uniform and other supplies online at scoutsdirect.org.nz.

What are the Costs to Join Scouts?

Costs vary from group to group so your local contact is the best one to advise you. In most cases there will be a registration fee to join the group and you will have to pay for your uniform. As the year progresses there may be extra costs associated with camping, special events, etc.

Is There Financial Aid For Families of Limited Means to Assist with the Costs of Joining Scouts?

Financial aid is available for families of limited means. Your group can assist or you can submit an application to the Scouts New Zealand Youth Foundation.

What Can My Child Expect When He/She Joins?

For information on Scouts New Zealand programs visit the Programs page. Your regional office will be happy to assist you with any specific questions you may have.

I Want to Learn About Becoming a Leader. What is the Process and What is Involved?

You can call 0800-SCOUTS, email join@scouts.org.nz or contact your regional office. You will need to be able:

  • To pass a police check
  • Undertake the required training

Where Do My Registration Fees Go?

For the 2013 Scouting year, $92.50 of the total registration fee goes to the Scouts New Zealand National Office to cover the cost of:

  • Training materials and resources for leaders
  • Planning programs and activities for youth (these must be planned meticulously to ensure compliance with insurance and health standards)
  • Publications and handbooks
  • Providing service and support to 38 zones across New Zealand
  • Volunteer servicing expenses
  • World Scouting fee
  • Risk Management

The remainder of the fee is levied by your local zone. Each zone is different, and in fact, fees differ from group to group. Your zone may need fees to provide service, support and programs to the groups and areas within their jurisdiction. Your zone takes into consideration these costs, and calculates their fee based on the lowest possible amount necessary to cover all costs.

Dollar for dollar, Scouts New Zealand’s programs provide significant value to its members. Programs run all year long and include many adventures such as camping trips that kids will remember for a lifetime. Most importantly, Scouting makes a difference in the lives of children, youth and young adults. They are more likely to demonstrate greater self-confidence and optimism about what the future holds. They express a strong sense of civic responsibility and have a desire to “pay back” the community. They become more physically fit, energetic, outgoing, conscientious and compassionate.

Does Scouts New Zealand Admit Both Boys and Girls?

Yes, Scouts New Zealand became completely co-ed in 1978.

Do You Have to Believe in God to Join Scouts New Zealand? Is Scouting a Christian Organization?

No, but you must have a basic spiritual belief. Spirituality has been one of the three main principles of Scouting around the world since its inception more than 100 years ago. Scouts New Zealand is proud of its commitment to diversity and welcomes members of many different faiths and denominations. You need not belong to an organized religion, but all members must take the Scout Promise in good faith and leaders may include some form of spirituality in their program for the youth. “God” represents spirituality and for some may represent an actual deity, but it may also mean an expression of your personal spirituality. "Duty to God" as defined by the World Organization of the Scouting Movement, means "a person's relationship with the spiritual values of life, the fundamental belief in a force above mankind."

Are Homosexuals Allowed to Join Scouts Canada?

Scouts Canada does not discriminate for reasons of gender, culture, religious belief or sexual orientation.
